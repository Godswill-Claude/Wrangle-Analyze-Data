Insights:¶

Analysis and visualization is one crucial but interesting part of the job of a Data Analyst. After gathering, assessing and cleaning our dataset, we try to look for insights - depending on what exactly we are trying to find or discover. 

Upon examining my cleaned master dataset, I decided to come up with the following insights:

Insight #1: Which dog stage has the highest number of tweets by tweeter users?

THE RESULT? I found out that pupper (a small doggo usually younger, inexperienced, unfamiliar and not ready for the responsibilities of being a doggo) has the highest number of likes or favourites. This comes as no surprise to me because people are naturally drawn to smaller, younger dogs - maybe because at this stage the dogs are usually more or less harmless and, more so, they can nuture them to adulthood and form a bond with them in the process

Insight #2: Which rating numerator of dogs occurs most frequently?

THE RESULT? 12 is the most frequently occuring dog rating numerator. This suggests that most persons are rather conservative with the rating of dogs. It would take an exceptional dog tweet to get a higher rating.

Insight #3: Does the stage of a dog determine the number of retweets by tweeter users?
